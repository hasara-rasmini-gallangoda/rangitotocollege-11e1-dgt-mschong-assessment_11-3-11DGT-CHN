Natural selection

Individuals with favourable phenotypes suited for their environment are likely to survive to reproduce.

Genetic drift

Genetic drift is the change in allele frequencies in populations due to chance events.

there are two types of genetic drift:

Bottleneck effect - When a significant drop in population occurs due to environmental events/human activity.

Founder effect - when a small number of individuals from a larger population,establish a new one.

this population would be reproductively isolated from the original population.

Definitions

Species: A group of individuals that can interbreed to produce fertile offspring.

Speciation: The formation of different species from a common ancestor/ancestral species.

Gene Flow: movement of alleles between populations/gene pools .

selection pressures: Factors that affect fitness/reproductive success.

Mutations: Random,permanent changes in the DNA base sequence.

Genetic Drift: random,permanent change in the DNA base sequence

Migration: When individuals move from one poulation to another.
